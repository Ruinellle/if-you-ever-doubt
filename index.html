Letter

I know the last weeks have felt confusing and heavy. I didn’t disappear because I stopped caring. I disappeared because my health pulled me under, and I didn’t know how to speak while drowning.

What I want you to know is simple: my intention toward you stayed clean. Even when my life looked messy. Even when I was afraid you’d be tired of hearing “hospital” again.

I’m not asking you to fix this instantly. I’m asking you to understand the difference between absence and indifference.

Always with respect,
— Malak
